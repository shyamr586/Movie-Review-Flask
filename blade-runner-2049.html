BLADE RUNNER 2049

Song: Mesa


IMDb rating: {{rating}}

My review


If there is a piece of art that is paralyzaingly beautiful, the closest movie would be Blade Runner 2049. This movie is a sequel to Blade Runner, and it follows a new blade runner, Officer K who has unearthed a long left secret that could ruin the society itself. His mission is to find ex-blade runner Rick Deckard (played by Harrison Ford) and find more about the secret. This movie is like no other. There have been many futuristic movies but none of them came close to be aesthetically pleasing as this movie did. The music in this movie is fantastic, composers Hanz Zimmer and Benjamin Wallfisch did a wonderful job setting the ambience throughout the movie. Speaking of ambience, cinematographer Roger Deakins presented us with this eyegasmic view of things in that futuristic world and the Oscar he got for it was well deserved. Lead actor playing the role of Officer K, Ryan Gosling did a great job of playing a helpless victim not knowing what to do. Unlike popular reamrks of actor Jared Leto, he absolutely nailed playing Wallace in this movie and has that god-like way of speaking and doing things. Over all this is a wonderful movie however I do not think it is everyone's cup of tea. This movie is a slow burn. And for a good reason at that. If people believed the movie would be packed with action sequences, they will be uninterested with this movie.





Spoiler Review


This movie for me is a 100% rewatchable. It is not because of the story but because of the feel it gives. Even the streets which can just be uninteresting naturally has never been more interesting in a movie ever. The sound effect of a car engine(?) pops up every 10 minutes or so and it gives off this cool-yet-suspensive vibe in all the scenes. I liked the character of Joi and her relationship with K. Upon viewing we see that K is not trapped literally but in a situation of helplessness as Joi literally tells you what you want to hear. If it weren't for that character, the movie would have been totally bland. Back to the cinematographer, I have no idea how he thought of this in his mind. The color pallete in almost every scene is unnatural like it's supposed to be and most importantly very eye-catching. Naturally, there are many scenes that I love re-watching the scene in which Officer K explores the ruins of Las Vegas which also shows the beauty of silence, the baseline test scenes (NOTE: VIEWER DISCRETION ADVICED) and Jared Leto's method acting.





Rating

Definitely a 10/10. Even while reviewing this movie it made me want to watch this movie all over again. Movies like these motivate people to make something atleast as remotely as close as this masterpiece.
